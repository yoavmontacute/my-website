Structure Meets Power 2026

Aim

There is a remarkable divide in the field of logic in Computer Science between two distinct strands: one focuses on semantics and compositionality (“Structure”), the other on expressiveness and complexity (“Power”).

We believe that bringing these communities together is an important objective. Previous workshops were held at LiCS 2021–2024.

This is a LiCS 2026 affiliated workshop. It will be a hybrid event, with on-site participation strongly encouraged.

Call for Participation

Those wishing to give a talk at the workshop are invited to submit an Extended Abstract of up to three pages (including references) describing the key points of the presentation. Submissions should only have a single author — the speaker. Any co-authors should be clearly credited in the abstract and on the slides.

For submissions please use the following Google form: TBA

We encourage talks at all levels of progress, including novel contributions, already published results, works in progress, as well as survey-type contributions. Depending on the number of submissions, contributed talks will be 20-30 minutes long.
